Heritage Site

Aluwihara Temple

Located near the iconic Sigiriya in Matale, Sri Lanka, the Alu Viharaya Temple adds a spiritual dimension to this historical landscape. Following a visit to the awe-inspiring Sigiriya rock fortress, venture to Alu Viharaya to delve into the island's religious heritage. The temple is renowned for its ancient cave complex, dating back to the 3rd century BC, and is a vital part of the Cultural Triangle, reflecting the confluence of Buddhism and history in the region. As you approach Alu Viharaya, you'll encounter a serene ambiance that contrasts with the grandeur of Sigiriya. The cave complex houses a series of carved Buddha statues and murals, offering a peaceful retreat for contemplation. The temple holds significance as a place where ancient Buddhist scriptures were once documented on ola leaves, contributing to the preservation of sacred teachings. The spiritual aura of Alu Viharaya complements the historical narrative of Sigiriya, providing visitors with a holistic experience that spans both cultural and religious dimensions.
