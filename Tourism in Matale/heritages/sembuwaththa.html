Sembuwatta Lake

Sembuwatta Lake (Sinhala: සෙම්ඹුවත්ත) is a tourist destination in Sri Lanka that is located next to the Campbell's Lane Forest Reserve in Elkaduwa, Matale District. A man-made lake made of natural spring water is called Sembuwatta Lake. There's a natural swimming pool along the lake. The estimated depth of Sembuwatta Lake is between 9 and 12 meters (39 and 30 feet). The Elkaduwa Plantations currently owns the lake, which provides power to the surrounding communities. Radley Dissage, the estate superintendent of Elkaduwa Plantations, refurbished the lake and its surroundings and linked it to neighboring water fountains.
